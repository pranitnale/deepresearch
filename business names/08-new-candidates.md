## Collision + language screens

### Mitrawerk — CLEAR (as compound)
- No exact hit for "Mitrawerk". Root users: **Mitra AI** (mitrai.com, AI-first legacy modernization, Mitra Innovation family) and many small "Mitra" firms; **MITRE** Corp is a phonetic neighbour (different vowel, defense/nonprofit). None in DACH, none using the compound.
- Language: German *die Mitra* = a bishop's mitre — a real German noun, neutral/ecclesiastical, not negative; "Mitra" also familiar in Europe via Latin/Persian *Mithra*. mitra = mitre in ES/IT/FR too. No rude meanings found in the 7-language screen.
- Pronunciation: MI-tra-verk — identical reading DE/EN, all open CV syllables, one-shot spelling.

### Kiranwerk — CLEAR (as compound)
- No exact hit for "Kiranwerk"/"Kiran Werk". Root users: Kiran Technologies (small Indian IT services, noted in 05); "Kiran" is otherwise a warm, familiar personal name in Europe.
- Language: no meaning in German or the other six languages; K plosive start (recall bonus per 01); KI-ran-verk reads identically in DE and EN.
- The -nix problem that killed Kiranix does not apply — "-werk" is the fix for the same root.

### Sumarga — CLEAR
- No company found using "Sumarga". Phonetic neighbours only: Suma Soft, SumatoSoft (both distinct in sound and spelling).
- Language: no German meaning; "Marga" is an old German first name (neutral). Faint echo of Spanish *amarga* ("bitter") — indirect, flagged for awareness; "su marga" in Spanish parses as "your marl" (obscure, neutral). Clean in FR/IT/NL/PL.
- Pronunciation: su-MAR-ga — every letter reads the same in German and English (initial S is /z/ in German, the Siemens effect — acceptable); pure CV syllables; one-shot spelling.

### Ojaswerk — CLEAR but second-tier
- No exact hit; ojas root confirmed open (only Ojas Audio NYC hi-fi, Ojas IT India, food brands).
- Demerit: the J shift (04 rule 2) — Germans say "O-yas-verk", English speakers "O-jass-werk". Two readings of the same name across your two main audiences.
