## The Tivran shape, generalized

What makes Tivran work by the rules: plosive T start + tight CVC ending → 2 syllables, 6 characters, one obvious spelling, coined/fanciful, sharp front-vowel sound matching tīvra's "intense" meaning. The family below varies the ending; the V caveat from 11 (German /f/ vs /v/, F/W misspelling risk — dictation test decisive) applies to every member.

Suffix note from the research: **-ex** is the classic corporate coined-name suffix (Xerox, Rolex, Timex tradition — legitimate per 01 rule 6); it is **-ix** that 05 flagged as comic-book (Krantix/Asterix). So Tivrex is rules-legal where Tivrix is not.

## Collision + rules screens

### Tivrin — VERDICT: CLEAR — new full-stack candidate
- **Collisions:** zero web hits for "Tivrin" — no company, no product, nothing.
- **Rules scorecard:** 2 syllables / 6 chars ✓; plosive start ✓; one-hearing spelling ✓ (TIV-rin — every letter audible); no meaning in the 7 screened languages ✓; fanciful ✓; sector-agnostic ✓.
- **Stylistic note:** faint pharma "-rin" echo (Aspirin family) — milder than Tivrium's "-ium".
- **.com + .ai + .de all available.**

### Tivrex — VERDICT: MINOR RISK (founder's suggestion — viable, `.ai`-first)
- **Collisions:** none as a company — the only "Tivrex" online is a Steam gaming profile. Corporate -ex suffix is rules-legal (see above).
- **Demerits:** tivrex.com is registered (resolves to a bare A record — parked-style; could be inquired for purchase); and the unavoidable **"T-rex" echo** — memorable, but slightly playful for a B2B parent brand. tivrex.ai + tivrex.de free.

### Tivren — CLEAR but .com taken
- No web hits; mild German-verb look ("-en" ending). tivren.ai/.de free.
