## Why tivra is a rules-compliant root

- **Plosive T start** (01 rule 2) and short length ✓
- **Sound–meaning congruence** (01 rules 3 + 11): the front vowel "i" sound-symbolically signals *fast, sharp, agile* — exactly what tīvra means. This is the rare root where the acoustics and the semantics agree.
- **Sector-agnostic** (01 rule 8): "intense/sharp" locks in neither AI nor energy.
- **Fanciful in Europe** (01 rule 6, 04 rule 8): no European-language meaning → strongest trademark tier.
- **The one structural weakness — the V** (04 rule 1): native German reads V as /f/ (Vogel, Vater), but Germans pronounce obvious loanwords/coinages with /v/ (Video, Vase, Vodafone), so a Tiv- coinage will mostly get /v/. Residual risk is in *spelling after one hearing* (F- or W- misspellings possible) — the dictation test (07 step 4) is decisive for this family.

## Collision + rules screens

### Tivrium — VERDICT: CLEAR — the rules-winner
- **Construction:** tivr- + Latinate element suffix *-ium* → reads as "the element of intensity" (lithium/helium family — an energy resonance without hard-coding energy, satisfying 01 rule 7).
- **Collisions:** zero hits for "Tivrium" anywhere.
- **Rules scorecard:** 3 syllables / 7 chars ✓; plosive start ✓; coined/fanciful ✓; sector-agnostic ✓; -ium is a familiar spelling chunk in all 7 screened languages, aiding one-hearing spelling ✓; no adverse meanings found ✓. Slight material-science/pharma flavor is the only stylistic note.
- **Say it:** TIV-ri-um (EN) / TIV-ri-um or TIF-ri-um (DE).
- **.com + .ai + .de all available (NXDOMAIN-confirmed).**

### Tivran — VERDICT: MINOR RISK
- **Best brevity of the family:** 2 syllables, 6 characters — the closest to the Kodak/Xerox shape.
- **Collisions:** a defunct e-commerce retailer "Tivran" still appears in search indexes at tivran.com, but the domain now returns NXDOMAIN (site dead, domain dropped — residual unregistered-trademark risk only). Near-name: **Tivian** (experience-management software, offices incl. Cologne) is one letter away in DACH B2B software.
- **Say it:** TIV-ran / TIF-ran.

### Tivrawerk — VERDICT: CLEAR but phonetically weakest of the -werk family
- Keeps the entire root visible + the proven *Werk* pattern; no collisions found.
- Demerit: the v-shift *and* the vr cluster make it the hardest -werk compound to say (DE "TIF-ra-verk" vs EN "TIV-ra-werk") — compare Tapanwerk's zero-shift phonetics (10).

### Ruled out BY THE RULES (despite full domain availability)
- **Tivrix** — "-ix" comic-book tone, the exact critique that killed Krantix (05).
- **Tivrus** — visual/aural echo of "virus"; bad B2B software association.
- **Tivrea** — no single obvious pronunciation (tiv-RAY-a / TIV-ree-a) → fails the one-hearing spelling test (04 rule 11).

### Dropped for collisions
